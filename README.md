# CYA1
This README will be a complete summary of my efforts. For the 500 word summary, please see SUMMARY.md.

For this adventure, I chose to recount my efforts to get an internship for Summer 2026. Since this is an assignment for a Spring class, I will only be going over my efforts since the start of this semester and won't be including anything from Fall. 

At the beginning of this semester, I set out to get an internship for Summer 2026. This furthers my career goals because it increases my chances of landing a job shortly after graduation. Before I get into the details of the steps that I took, I will provide some background on who I am. I'm Evan Bay, a Junior studying Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics. I studied at Portland Community College for one year and transferred to Washington State University as a Sophomore. I have a background and passion in game development and have recently been elected for President of the Game Development Club which puts on multiple semesterly events and projects, along with a yearly event hosting 100+ students. I have focused on games during my career largely because I enjoy playing them and thinking about them. During my time here at WSU, I have come to realize I would be satisfied doing anything that has an impact on other people. I greatly enjoy designing experiences for others which is part of the reason I think I enjoy game development and operating the game development club so much. I believe I could become passionate about anything as long as there is always something to learn and people to design for. 

Now I'll get into how my search will be broken down. During the semester, I have had 3 different resumes and 2 different portfolios. I will be reviewing all of my activity between each resume change since it usually marks a burst in applications and preparation. I'll be estimating the time spent during each burst based on Claude's estimations of each task on average (average time to apply to a job, average time to complete a leetcode question, etc.) since I have not logged my time for these activites myself. I will also be reviewing the changes between resumes and each resume's strengths and weaknesses. This assignment will be taken as an opportunity to review and reflect on my job search to hopefully improve in the future and be able to help others with their search. 

## January
During January (Specifically January 15-26 (exlcuding activity before the semester began)), I applied to around 30 internships. During this time, I used a job tracking website Simplify to keep track of where I have applied. I have since deleted a decent amount of my applications to clear my search board but I have recently looked through my emails for application confirmation responses and have estimated around 30.

Here is the breakdown of the results:
- Rejected by 13
- Ghosted by 15
- Moved to Online Assessment by 1
- Withdrew from 1

Total time spent: 7.5 hours (estimated ~15 minutes per application)

To prepare for any technical interviews I might get during this time, I did 7 leetcode problems.

Here is the breakdown of problems done:
- 1 Easy
- 4 Medium
- 2 Hard

Total time spent: 4.25 hours (estimated ~15 minutes for Easy, ~30 minutes for Medium, and ~1 hr for hard)

The online assessment was from ZipRecruiter and was the first online assessment I've done. It was hosted through CodeSignal which luckily lets me review the scores of previous assessments. For the ZipRecruiter assessment, I got scored 350/600. This means I was unable to get some test cases to pass. If I recall correctly, this assessment had two parts. The first was easier and took about 10-20 minutes. The second part was about matrix maniupulation. I had to implement functions to reorder rows and columns of matrices and eventually rotate a matrix clockwise. Since I was unable to finish the clockwise rotation, most tests failed even though the rest of my funcions were fully implemented. After this assessment, I realized how much I needed to be doing more leetcode and practice technical questions if I ever wanted to pass online assessments. After this, I started doing CodeSignal practice problems and more leetcode.

The withdraw was from Roblox. I simply never got around to doing their online assessment which was done through a Roblox game.

Applications slowed down a bit after this period because I joined a week-long game jam and made ProtoPunk which is now on my current resume. The February WSU Career Expo was also coming up so I was preparing for that as well.

The personality assessment was given by Veeva Systems and was a bit strange and unrelated to the job. Personality assessment is important for candidates and companies but this assessment mixed in word association with a "correct" answer, and unrelated multiple choice options such as "which is more like you? (is organized) (takes initiative)". Ultimately, I was rejected from this position.

The first online assessment was administered by IBM. I don't remember much about this assessment but I didn't do too well and ended up getting rejected. The second online assessment was from Amazon where I got two greedy algorithm questions and did not move on to the next round.

Fortunately, I had applied to SEL on March 2nd and they got back to me around MArch 20th for a phone call screening. It was pretty standard behavioral, asking about times I encountered specific situations. I got a call a few weeks later about scheduling an in-person interview which happened April 1st. To my surprise, there was no technical portion but it did feature some technical concepts asked when discussing my resume. I was interviewed by a panel of 5 and mostly asked about my resume, how I dealt with certain situations, and it lasted around 2 hours. Around 2 weeks later, they called to tell me they were moving forward with another candidate.
